Mohinga Recipe

Mohinga is a traditional Burmese dish often considered the national dish of Myanmar. It is a flavorful fish soup served over rice noodles and garnished with a variety of toppings. The broth is typically made with fish, lemongrass, ginger, and garlic, creating a fragrant and savory experience. Mohinga is commonly enjoyed for breakfast but can be eaten at any time of the day.

Ingredients

  • Rice noodles (8 ounces)
  • Fish (catfish or any white fish, about 1 pound)
  • Water (6 cups)
  • Lemongrass (2 stalks, chopped)
  • Ginger (1-inch piece, sliced)
  • Garlic (4 cloves, minced)
  • Onion (1 medium, chopped)
  • Fish sauce (3 tablespoons)
  • Turmeric powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Chickpea flour (2 tablespoons)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • Boiled eggs (for garnish)
  • Cilantro (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)
  • Fried garlic or shallots (for garnish)
  • Chili flakes (optional, for garnish)

Steps to make Mohinga

  1. Prepare the Broth:
    • In a large pot, combine water, fish, lemongrass, ginger, garlic, and onion. Bring to a boil and then simmer for about 20 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
    • Remove the fish, let it cool, and then flake it into pieces.
    • Strain the broth to remove the solids and return the liquid to the pot.
  2. Make the Soup:
    • In a small bowl, mix the chickpea flour with a little water to form a paste.
    • Add this to the strained broth along with turmeric, fish sauce, salt, and pepper.
    • Stir well and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  3. Cook the Noodles:
    • Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
    • Drain and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Dish:
    • In serving bowls, place a portion of the cooked rice noodles.
    • Ladle the hot fish soup over the noodles.
  5. Garnish:
    • Top with flaked fish, boiled eggs, cilantro, fried garlic or shallots, and chili flakes if desired.
    • Serve with lime wedges on the side.
